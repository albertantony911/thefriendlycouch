WHAT IS DIAGNOSTIC ASSESSMENT ?

WHAT IS DIAGNOSTIC ASSESSMENT ?

Understanding and finding clarity about mental health disorders can make a big difference in your well-being. We've made sure this process is as simple as possible. Our team of RCI Registered Clinical Psychologists use an objective and evidence-based process to provide you with a diagnosis or to rule out any disorders.

We use standardised tests & assessments and follow the International Classification of Diseases (ICD-10) guidelines to give you official diagnoses for mental health disorders.

If you're considering a diagnostic assessment, here are some things you can expect:

CLARITY

If you're unsure whether you're dealing with a mental health disorder, a diagnostic assessment can help you gain clarity.

OFFICIAL DIAGNOSIS

Receiving an official diagnosis from a trained & qualified mental health expert is a pivotal step in understanding and navigating through the disorder.

RE-EVALUATION

If you've previously received a diagnosis but have doubts or questions, a diagnostic assessment with one of our Clinical Psychologists can offer a fresh perspective.

DIAGNOSTIC ASSESSMENT PROCESS

Here's a comprehensive step-by-step overview of how we conduct the diagnostic assessment process.

1

UNDERSTANDING YOUR STORY

In the first session, we'll spend time understanding your concerns. This includes a detailed history-taking session and a mental status examination. It typically lasts for 1 hour.

2

PSYCHOMETRIC TEST

The second session is all about checking symptoms through testing. We use a set of standardised psychometric assessments that are evidence-based and widely recognised. This session typically lasts for about 1 to 2 hours*. These assessments, combined with the information from the first session, provide a clearer picture of your mental health.

CHOOSE THE RIGHT THERAPIST?

( UNDERSTAND THE DIFFERENCE )

We understand that often mental health professionals seem similar and picking the right one can be confusing. The psychologists on our team have distinct roles and we're here to help you understand and guide you in choosing the correct therapist for your requirements. All the Psychologists on our team are hired after an intensive review and interview process. You can trust the Psychologists on our team to be non-judgemental and professional.

    COMMON QUESTIONS

    We usually suggest going to a counselling psychologist if you’re new to therapy. In case you need specialised help, you can always be transferred to the clinical psychologist through our internal transfer process. However, if you are already diagnosed or would like to get diagnosed or have suicidal thoughts, or think you might have a disorder please opt for a clinical psychologist.

    You are welcome to prepare some points if you wish, but there is no obligation to do so before the session.

    We typically recommend starting with a counseling psychologist if you're new to therapy. If you require specialized assistance, you can be transferred to a clinical psychologist through our internal process. However, if you have already been diagnosed, are seeking a diagnosis, have suicidal thoughts, or suspect you may have a disorder, we strongly advise opting for a clinical psychologist.

    Therapy is a process, and expecting a cure in one session is not realistic. The initial session focuses on building a therapeutic relationship and understanding your main concerns. Just as problems don't manifest overnight and evolve over time, the healing process also requires time and cannot be completed in a single session. Each individual is unique, and their healing journey will unfold at its own pace.

    It's important to note that our therapists are not crisis counselors and cannot communicate with you outside of scheduled sessions. This policy is in place to prevent dependency and to uphold their professional boundaries and timings.

    We do not offer chat-based therapy. Chat therapy is not included in the training of academic psychologists, and effective therapy cannot be adequately conducted through messages or chat. We strongly advise against opting for therapy services via chat, as this method is not scientifically proven for counseling and may not provide the intended benefits.

    There is no age limit for consulting a psychologist on our team. For minors (under 18), we require a consent letter or acknowledgment from an adult regarding the minor's therapy. The consenting adult does not necessarily have to be a parent or guardian but should be someone capable of providing consent and acknowledging the client's participation in therapy. If you are able to provide a consent letter, please let us know, and we will provide the necessary details for inclusion in the letter.
